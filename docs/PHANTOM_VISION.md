# Vision

PhantomForce is not a chatbot, a dashboard, or a loose pile of AI tools. PhantomForce is a private operating intelligence for a business owner: an adaptive operator brain with controlled hands and feet.

The product should feel like the business has a living command center. It watches the work, understands the business, organizes signals, proposes decisions, prepares execution, remembers what matters, and keeps the owner in control.

The long-term vision is simple and extremely ambitious: a business owner should feel like PhantomForce is the central nervous system of the company.

# Product Transformation

PhantomForce must move from "AI tool suite" to "operating intelligence."

That means:

- The UI is not the product.
- Chat is not the product.
- The LLM is reasoning tissue, not the company.
- Memory, Hermes, ledgers, signals, approvals, context, tools, workers, automations, Media Lab, Content Hub, websites, analytics, clients, money, and security are the system.
- Workers and tools are hands and feet.
- Approvals, permissions, dry-runs, proof logs, and tenant boundaries are impulse control.
- The owner should not hunt for work. Work should arrive.

Every feature should strengthen the operating loop: observe, understand, decide, prepare, approve, execute, prove, remember, and improve.

# Core User Experience

PhantomForce should feel premium, alive, calm, and useful. It should not feel like a cluttered SaaS dashboard, a tab graveyard, or an AI-generated cyber panel.

The default experience should be minimal until summoned. The user should be able to ask normally, see what matters, and move work forward without learning developer language.

The system should separate:

- Owner/admin control.
- Employee permissions.
- Customer-facing workspaces.
- Internal developer/backend tools.
- Public marketing surfaces.

Normal users should see outcomes, decisions, tasks, assets, automations, approvals, analytics, and business records. They should not need to see Apify, n8n, provider names, raw tool lanes, scaffolds, environment variables, or backend implementation details.

# Command Center

The command center is the primary operating surface. It should not behave like a dumb command prompt. It should feel smart: the user states an outcome, a problem, a question, or a goal, and PhantomForce decides the correct lane.

The command center must:

- Accept casual chat without turning every message into a task.
- Detect explicit work requests.
- Route work to the right department, worker, tool, or decision card.
- Use memory and business context.
- Show short, useful, human responses.
- Prepare drafts, plans, assets, automations, proposals, pages, analysis, or approvals.
- Keep external actions gated.
- Explain when something needs setup without exposing unnecessary backend names.

The command center is not just "chat." It is the front door to the operating system.

# Outcomes

PhantomForce should organize work around outcomes, not app features.

Examples:

- Get more leads.
- Follow up with warm prospects.
- Generate five content ideas today.
- Build a landing page.
- Improve a video.
- Review security posture.
- Prepare outreach.
- Check analytics.
- Protect the business.
- Keep work moving while the owner is away.

An Outcome should have:

- Goal.
- Current status.
- Related signals.
- Suggested decisions.
- Required approvals.
- Assigned department/workers.
- Evidence.
- Progress history.
- Next best action.

The system should turn fuzzy intent into visible business progress.

# Workforce

The workforce is the visible metaphor for internal capability. Workers, subagents, departments, automations, and tools should feel like a coordinated business operations network, not a fake list of pretend employees.

Workforce must be honest:

- Real connected services should show as live.
- Baseline capacity can show as mapped or available.
- Subagents can represent capability lanes.
- Fake activity must not be presented as real work.
- Worker counts should distinguish real active runs from mapped capacity.

The workforce should make complex systems understandable through graphics, maps, status, and drill-downs. Click a worker to expand what it can do, what it has done, what it needs, and what it is waiting on.

# Signals

Signals are observations that may matter to the business.

Signals can come from:

- Clients.
- Leads.
- Social analytics.
- Content performance.
- Competitor intelligence.
- Security scans.
- Local system health.
- Media and asset activity.
- Website changes.
- Automations.
- Worker runs.
- Owner corrections.
- Approvals.
- Money movement.

Signals are not decisions by themselves. They feed decisions. Signals need source, timestamp, confidence, scope, and evidence.

# Decision Cards

Decision Cards are how PhantomForce turns signals into action without overwhelming the owner.

A Decision Card should answer:

- What happened?
- Why does it matter?
- What is the recommended move?
- What evidence supports it?
- What risk exists?
- What will happen if approved?
- What will not happen without approval?

Decision Cards should be concise, specific, and actionable. They should be the bridge between intelligence and execution.

# Institutional Memory

Memory is not random chat history. Memory is institutional knowledge.

PhantomForce should remember:

- Durable business facts.
- User preferences.
- Brand rules.
- Client context.
- Project patterns.
- Corrections.
- Tool state.
- Approval policy.
- Media style.
- Known avoidances.
- Repeated failure patterns.

Temporary history should expire. Durable memory should be editable, forgettable, tenant-scoped, and safe. The owner should be able to inspect what PhantomForce knows and why it used a memory.

Institutional memory should help the system stop repeating known mistakes.

# Planner

The planner should make work arrive.

It should unify:

- Daily priorities.
- Follow-ups.
- Scheduled content.
- Automations.
- Approvals.
- Client work.
- Website work.
- Media work.
- Security work.
- Money/accounting work.

The planner is not just a calendar. It is the operating queue for the business.

# Analytics

Analytics must show real business intelligence, not empty developer disclaimers.

When live feeds are connected, metrics should be visual, colorful, readable, and decision-oriented. When they are not connected, setup should be clear and honest.

Analytics should connect to:

- Social accounts.
- Content performance.
- Website/store performance.
- Money.
- Leads.
- Campaigns.
- Competitor signals.
- Worker/automation throughput.

Analytics should produce decisions, not just charts.

# Intelligence

Intelligence means evidence before opinion.

PhantomForce should gather and organize lawful, legitimate signals. It should not fabricate data or imply unauthorized access. Competitor intelligence, security checks, CRM discovery, and market research must remain source-backed and clearly labeled by confidence.

The intelligence layer should:

- Track sources.
- Date evidence.
- State confidence.
- Offer alternate explanations.
- Recommend verification.
- Suggest lawful business responses.

# Away Mode

Away Mode is controlled coverage while the owner is unavailable.

It should:

- Watch for important signals.
- Continue approved low-risk work.
- Queue risky actions.
- Surface exceptions.
- Stop instantly when turned off.
- Never send, post, spend, upload, deploy, delete, or mutate external systems without permitted approval.

Away Mode should feel like trusted coverage, not runaway automation.

# Design Direction

PhantomForce should look and feel like a premium command operating system:

- Dark luxury OS.
- Clean and modern.
- Green/cyan energy.
- Smooth motion.
- Minimal until summoned.
- Alive but not childish.
- Practical before decorative.
- Mobile-first where operators actually use it.

Visuals should communicate system state. Animation should tell a story. The Phantom character should feel useful, subtle, and alive, not a giant mascot blocking work.

# Definition of Victory

PhantomForce wins when a business owner can open the app and immediately feel:

- "It knows my business."
- "It knows what matters today."
- "It tells me what needs a decision."
- "It can prepare the work."
- "It keeps me in control."
- "It remembers how I work."
- "It makes my business harder to compete with."

The end state is not more tabs. The end state is a private adaptive business operator that routes work safely, proves what happened, and compounds knowledge over time.
